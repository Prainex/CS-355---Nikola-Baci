Martin Luther King Jr.

Martin Luther King Jr. was a leader in the Civil Rights Movement, advocating for nonviolent resistance and equality for African Americans.

He is best known for his role in the March on Washington in 1963, where he delivered his iconic "I Have a Dream" speech, envisioning a racially integrated and harmonious America.

In 1964, he was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his efforts to combat racial inequality through nonviolent means.
